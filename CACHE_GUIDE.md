# CACHE_GUIDE.md

## Purpose
This file explains each cache type in simple language so the app can teach users what they are cleaning.

## Cache type format
For each type, use:
- What it is.
- Why it exists.
- Why clean it.
- Possible side effects.

## Browser cache
- What it is: Stored website images, scripts, and page files.
- Why it exists: Makes websites load faster.
- Why clean it: Helps when a site looks broken, outdated, or slow.
- Side effects: Websites may load a little slower the first time after cleanup.

## Cookies and site data
- What it is: Login sessions, preferences, and site-specific settings.
- Why it exists: Keeps you signed in and remembers choices.
- Why clean it: Helps with login problems or corrupted site state.
- Side effects: You may be signed out of sites.

## Temporary files
- What it is: Short-lived files created by apps and installers.
- Why it exists: Helps software work during installation or editing.
- Why clean it: Frees space and removes leftover junk.
- Side effects: Usually low risk.

## Windows Update cache
- What it is: Downloaded update files stored by Windows.
- Why it exists: Supports update installation and retries.
- Why clean it: Helps when updates are stuck or storage is low.
- Side effects: Windows may re-download update files.

## DNS cache
- What it is: Saved website name-to-IP lookups.
- Why it exists: Speeds up network resolution.
- Why clean it: Helps when websites fail to open because of stale DNS data.
- Side effects: None important; the cache rebuilds quickly.

## Microsoft Store cache
- What it is: Store download and app installation cache.
- Why it exists: Helps the Store work faster.
- Why clean it: Useful when Store apps fail to download or update.
- Side effects: The Store may open a bit slower the first time.

## Thumbnail cache
- What it is: Small preview images for files and folders.
- Why it exists: Makes folders open faster with previews.
- Why clean it: Helps when thumbnails are broken or outdated.
- Side effects: Thumbnails will regenerate.

## Icon cache
- What it is: Saved file and app icons.
- Why it exists: Makes icons load quickly.
- Why clean it: Fixes missing or incorrect icons.
- Side effects: Icons rebuild after cleanup.

## Prefetch cache
- What it is: Data Windows uses to help programs start faster.
- Why it exists: Improves app launch performance.
- Why clean it: Useful when the cache is stale or corrupted.
- Side effects: Some apps may launch a bit slower at first.

## GPU and shader cache
- What it is: Compiled graphics data used by games and graphics apps.
- Why it exists: Reduces stutter and speeds rendering.
- Why clean it: Helps when games glitch or shaders are corrupted.
- Side effects: Games may recompile shaders and stutter briefly afterward.

## App-specific cache
- What it is: Temporary data stored by a specific app.
- Why it exists: Improves app performance and responsiveness.
- Why clean it: Fixes app bugs, old data, and storage bloat.
- Side effects: App may rebuild data and take longer the first time.

## Log files
- What it is: Diagnostic records written by apps and system components.
- Why it exists: Helps with troubleshooting.
- Why clean it: Frees space when logs become too large.
- Side effects: Older troubleshooting history may be removed.

## Safety note
Do not describe every cached file as trash. Some cache helps the system run faster, so cleanup should be selective and explained carefully.
